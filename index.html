Project: The Card-less Future


Overview

For the past couple of years, one of the buzz words that has been thrown around was Cryptocurrency, an online currency that has value in the real world, recently Apple has announced the Apple Card, a physical card for all types of currency from all types of banks to be integrated into their apple pay system. Everything is slowly becoming digitalised from the way we are able to purchase products with the touch of our phone or smart watch, to a few types of identification factors such as the health care card being integrated into the Centrelink app, what if we were able to step outside one day and not have to worry about bringing our wallet as our credit card, student id, driver’s license, gym membership, mkyi were on an app where all we need is our phone in order to access those items, a card-less future.




Motivation

According to the Statista, the expected number of smart phone owners are roughly around 2.7 billion people, with an expected number of mobile phone users to surpass 5 billion by 2019. This is more than half the current population of the world. An average person leaving their house to travel into the CBD would on average bring along in their wallet; money, credit card, any sort of identification and a concession card, imagine a future where one day all they would need to bring their mobile phone as all these miscellaneous items would all be integrated into an app. Not only would we be saving money on printing the cards, but the removing the hassle of adding carrying around a wallet which would increase the chances of it being lost or stolen, where if the only thing you had to worry about was just your phone and your keys.



statista

Description

The Application would ideally start out on both iOS and android devices but will slowly make its way towards every possible mobile device. The application would aim to replace all the cards we use now, from identification, credit cards and membership cards with the purpose to transferring all these physical cards onto our mobile devices. The application would be similar to the current Wallet app found on iOS, where the user is required to manually enter the information required for the card or quite simply taking a photo of it, this will then add the desired card onto the device. While currently Apple’s app only works on credit cards, movie tickets and coupons, this application will take the idea further with the addition of all possible cards. Specific Cards would also have special functions built within the application. For example the driver’s license, at the moment if you were to change your address, you would have to log onto Vic Roads, change to your new address and wait 2 weeks for them to send you a sticker so that you can stick onto the back of your driver’s licence, with this app, you can change you address on the fly without having to resort to sticking a small piece of paper on the back of the physical card. Another card specific feature would be the Mkyi card, imagine if you were able to top up your myki card on the fly through the app, instead of using the outdated top up machines which works about half the time.

Tools and Technology

As this is mainly a phone application, using software which is designed for uses on mobile phones would be ideal. Software required would be those in which would allow for coding specifically for mobile phones, ideally for both iOS and Android as the application is looking to be used cross platform. Creative Apps would also be required in design how the application itself would look and run, as user experience is also a major factor in this, as the application needs to be able to store a wide range of different cards. Software such as .NET to build cross-platform applications and JavaScript for developing the application, using Adobe Photoshop, After Effects and Illustrator for the design of the application and additional software’s such as Swift, CoreData and Apple XC test and SQL to assist in programming the application.

Skills Required

As stated above for the required applications and software, having knowledge of all the programming language would be very helpful, as some application would require coding in Java while others would require C++, also skills in using the Adobe programs would be required. Knowledge on user experience would also be very helpful, as no matter how useful the application is, if the user cannot figure out how to use it, it will ultimately be useless, therefore knowledge of user experience to create a good user interface would be required. Most of the applications required are either free on the internet or provided by the university itself, however the skills required is not quite feasible. As the application will be handling important information of the user, a professional level of skills is required for the software development to minimise possible bugs that could ultimately leak user info or put the user’s data at risk.

Outcome

If the project becomes successful, we are one step closer to a digital future, as the woes of carrying credit cards, money, identification and train tickets can all be voided as all we need is our mobile phone to complete all those tasks. From purchasing items, to tapping on and off to needing to show your identification all being done from one device. The convince of being able to update your personal details and having it update immediately, not having to deal with expired credit cards and concession cards, topping up your myki card and paying for everything with your phone and not have to deal with loose change or carry large amounts of money. With the implementation of this application we are one step close to a fully digitised future.
